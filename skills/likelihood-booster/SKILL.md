# Perceived Likelihood Booster

Audit what increases your prospect's belief that they will actually achieve the dream outcome, identify gaps in your proof stack, and produce a prioritized plan for building the evidence that commands premium prices.

Read `references/frameworks/value-equation-explained.md` for how perceived likelihood fits into the Value Equation.

## Why This Matters

Perceived likelihood of achievement is the second variable in Hormozi's Value Equation, and it's the one that lets you charge premium prices for the exact same service. The classic example:

> "If you're thinking about getting liposuction and there's one surgeon that's just fresh out of medical school, hasn't done a surgery yet, and there's another physician who's got 10,000 five-star surgeries under his belt — who do you go to? The guy with 10,000. It's the same procedure, but the perceived likelihood that you're going to get what you want is significantly higher, and so you pay for that premium."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

The inverse of perceived likelihood is risk. Every element that increases belief decreases risk. Guarantees (covered in `/guarantee-designer`) are the explicit mechanism. But proof is the implicit mechanism that does the heavy lifting before you ever need to mention a guarantee.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Audit Current Proof Assets

Ask the user to list every piece of evidence they currently use to support their claims:

1. **Testimonials:** Written, video, or audio? How many? How specific are the results mentioned?
2. **Case studies:** Detailed before/after with numbers? Or just "John loved working with us"?
3. **Credentials:** Certifications, years of experience, number of clients served, industry recognition?
4. **Data:** Can you cite specific averages, percentages, or aggregate results across your customer base?
5. **Media/Press:** Featured anywhere? Quoted by anyone?
6. **Speed evidence:** How quickly do first results appear? Can you prove it?

### Step 2: Score Against the Proof Hierarchy

Hormozi's implicit hierarchy (reconstructed from multiple episodes) ranks proof by persuasive power:

| Tier | Proof Type | Power | Example |
|------|-----------|-------|---------|
| **S-Tier** | Aggregate data with specific numbers | Highest | "Average client adds $240K/year, 3.1x profit increase" |
| **A-Tier** | Before/after case studies with metrics | High | "Sarah went from $2,950/mo to $8,900/mo in 90 days" |
| **B-Tier** | Video testimonials (emotional + specific) | Medium-High | Customer on camera describing their transformation |
| **C-Tier** | Written testimonials with specifics | Medium | Screenshot reviews with numbers attached |
| **D-Tier** | Credentials and experience counts | Low-Medium | "10,000 surgeries" or "turned around 33 gyms" |
| **F-Tier** | Self-claims without evidence | Lowest | "We're the best in the industry" |

Most businesses operate at C-Tier or below. The gap between D-Tier and S-Tier is where pricing power lives.

> "I sold 4,000 gym memberships before I ever taught anyone how to sell. And so I knew what I was talking about because I had done it so many times... the depth of the knowledge, the roots were so deep, it was really impossible to compare my experience to anyone else's."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Create A GRAND SLAM Offer"

### Step 3: Gap Analysis

For each tier the user is missing, identify:

1. **What data do you already have but aren't using?** (Most businesses sit on proof they never surface. Check your customer success stories, internal metrics, survey results.)
2. **What proof could you create in the next 30 days?** (Video testimonial batch, case study write-ups, aggregate metric calculations)
3. **What proof requires time to build?** (Track record, certifications, published results)

### Step 4: Build the Proof Stack Plan

Map the user's sales context to the right proof deployment:

| Sales Context | Lead With | Support With | Close With |
|--------------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Sales call (high-ticket)** | Aggregate data claim | 2-3 specific case studies | Guarantee |
| **Sales page (self-serve)** | Headline with specific number | Video testimonials | Social proof count |
| **Cold outreach** | One specific result | Credential | CTA to case study |
| **Referral/warm** | Mutual connection result | Broader data | Simple ask |
