# Offer Namer & Packager

Generate naming options using Hormozi's naming criteria, then package the full offer into a presentable format with offer stack, guarantee, bonuses, and price anchoring language — ready for a sales page, pitch deck, or sales script.

Read `references/frameworks/naming-psychology.md` for the full naming methodology and examples.

## Naming Methodology

Hormozi's naming formula for lead magnets (which extends to offers) follows a specific pattern:

**[Result] + [Timeframe] + [Container]**

Examples:
- "6-Week Shred Challenge" (result: shredded, timeframe: 6 weeks, container: challenge)
- "30-Day Revenue Sprint" (result: revenue, timeframe: 30 days, container: sprint)
- "90-Minute Offer Audit" (result: audited offer, timeframe: 90 minutes, container: audit)

The name should do three things:
1. **Promise a specific result** — not vague improvement, a measurable outcome
2. **Compress time** — the timeframe should feel fast relative to the result
3. **Signal the format** — the container word tells them what they're getting

> "Marketing is about different. Business is about better."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Create A GRAND SLAM Offer"

The name is the marketing. It must be different from everything else in the market. The delivery is the business — that must be better.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Gather All Offer Components

If the user has run previous skills, compile their outputs. Otherwise, ask for:

1. **Dream outcome:** What result does the customer achieve?
2. **Proof/likelihood:** What evidence supports this result?
3. **Time to result:** How fast do they get it?
4. **Effort required:** What do they have to do?
5. **Guarantee:** What risk reversal are you offering?
6. **Bonuses:** What additional value items are included?
7. **Scarcity/urgency:** What limits exist?
8. **Target customer:** Who is this for specifically?

### Step 2: Generate Name Options

Create 5-7 naming options across different structures:

| Structure | Formula | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Result + Time + Container** | [outcome] in [timeframe] [container] | "Revenue Machine in 90 Days Accelerator" |
| **Avatar + Transformation** | The [avatar] [transformation program] | "The Agency Owner's Client Acquisition System" |
| **Proprietary Method** | The [branded name] Method/System/Framework | "The Grand Slam Offer Blueprint" |
| **Challenge/Sprint** | [Timeframe] [Result] [Challenge/Sprint] | "21-Day Offer Overhaul Sprint" |
| **Provocative** | [Bold claim] [Container] | "The Only Marketing Program You'll Ever Need" |

**Naming checklist:**
- Does it communicate the dream outcome in the name itself?
- Does it sound different from every competitor's offer?
- Is there a timeframe that compresses perceived time delay?
- Does the container word match the delivery format?
- Is it memorable enough to refer a friend?
- Does it pass the "cocktail party test" — can someone describe it in one sentence?

### Step 3: Package the Offer Stack

Assemble all components into a sales-ready format:

**The stack structure (for sales page or pitch deck):**

1. **Headline:** Dream outcome + timeframe + proof point
2. **The problem:** What they're struggling with (their words)
3. **The solution:** Your offer (named) with the core mechanism
4. **The value equation:** How your offer maximizes all 4 variables
5. **Social proof:** Best 3-5 proof assets from `/likelihood-booster`
6. **The stack:** Core offer + each bonus with perceived value
7. **The guarantee:** From `/guarantee-designer`
8. **Scarcity/urgency:** From `/scarcity-urgency-builder`
9. **The price:** Anchored against the total stack value
10. **The close:** Clear CTA with one action

### Step 4: Write Price Anchoring Language

Hormozi's price presentation method (from `/scarcity-urgency-builder` and the pricing transcripts):

**The anchor-high technique:**
If your target price is $3,000, present $4,000 as the sticker price with a prepayment option that lands at $3,000.

> "The price that we are going to be anchoring is not the price that we're actually trying to get to. So if we want to get to $3,000 from $2,000, we're actually going to talk about a $4,000 price point, which anchors high. And then when we introduce the prepayment discount, that is actually the number that we're looking for."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How I RAISE PRICES"

**Price anchoring language formula:**
1. State the total perceived value of the stack ($XX,XXX)
2. State the anchor price ($X,XXX)
3. Introduce the payment option (anchor price split)
4. Offer the prepay discount (target price)
