# Affiliate & Referral System Designer

Design a program that gets other people to bring you leads and customers - customer referrals, affiliate partnerships, agency relationships, or employee sales teams - using Hormozi's "get others to advertise for you" framework.

Read `references/frameworks/100m-leads-core-four.md` for how referrals and affiliates fit into the Core Four.

## Why This Matters

Hormozi's Core Four advertising framework splits into "you do it" and "others do it." The "others" category has four sub-channels:

1. **Customer referrals** - Happy customers tell their friends
2. **Affiliates** - Other businesses/creators promote you for a commission
3. **Agencies** - You hire someone to run your advertising for you
4. **Employees** - You build a sales team

> "There's really only eight ways to let other people know about the stuff you sell. Four of those ways, you do yourself. Four of those ways, other people do for you."
> — Alex Hormozi, "8 Ways To Get Customers"

Most businesses rely on accidental referrals. Hormozi's approach turns referrals into a system with predictable output.

The key insight: referrals are not a marketing tactic. They are a product quality metric. If you are not getting referrals, your product or experience has a gap.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Assess Referral Readiness

Ask the user:

1. **How many active customers do you have?**
2. **What is your Net Promoter Score (or: do customers voluntarily refer you already)?**
3. **What happens after a customer finishes your program/service?** (Do they just leave?)
4. **What is the lifetime value of a customer?**
5. **What could you afford to pay per referred customer?**

**Kill switch:** If they have fewer than 10 happy customers, they are not ready for a formal referral program. The product needs more proof first. Send them to `/warm-outreach-system`.

### Step 2: Choose the Right "Others" Channel

| Channel | Prerequisites | Best For | Typical Cost |
|---------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| **Customer referrals** | Happy customers exist | Any business | $0-100 per referral (incentive) |
| **Affiliates** | Proven offer + tracking system | Digital products, courses, SaaS | 20-50% commission |
| **Agency partnership** | Budget + clear KPIs | Paid ads, SEO, content | Retainer + % of spend |
| **Employee sales team** | Documented sales process | High-ticket, volume sales | Base + commission |

For most businesses under $1M/year, customer referrals are the only "others" channel worth building. Affiliate programs require infrastructure. Agency hires require budget. Sales teams require documented processes.

### Step 3: Design the Referral Incentive

The incentive must be worth the effort of making the referral. Hormozi's principle: make the referral rewarding for both sides.

| Incentive Type | How It Works | Best For |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| **Double-sided discount** | Referrer gets X, referred gets Y | Subscriptions, memberships |
| **Cash/credit reward** | Referrer gets $ per conversion | High-ticket services |
| **Upgraded access** | Referrer gets premium features | SaaS, communities |
| **Recognition** | Referrer gets public acknowledgment | Status-driven audiences |
| **Tiered rewards** | More referrals = bigger rewards | Power referrers |

**Pricing the incentive:**
- Customer LTV: $X
- Acquisition cost via other channels: $Y
- Referral incentive should be: $Y x 0.5 to $Y x 0.75 (cheaper than other channels)

### Step 4: Build the Ask System

Referrals do not happen passively. You must ask, and you must ask at the right time.

**The 3 Referral Moments:**

| Moment | When | Why It Works | How to Ask |
|--------|------|-------------|------------|
| **Peak happiness** | Right after they get a result | Emotion is highest | "Who else do you know who would want this result?" |
| **Onboarding** | During first week/interaction | Excitement is fresh | "If this works for you, would you share it with a friend?" |
| **Milestone** | After a specific achievement | Concrete proof to share | "You just hit [milestone]. Would you be open to sharing this?" |

Build the ask into your operational process, not as an afterthought. Put it on a calendar or in an automation.

### Step 5: Build the Tracking System

| What to Track | Why | Tool |
|--------------|-----|------|
| Who referred whom | Calculate per-referrer value | CRM or spreadsheet |
| Referral conversion rate | Optimize the program | Referral software or manual |
| Time from referral to close | Measure quality of referred leads | CRM |
| Incentive cost per acquisition | Ensure profitability | Accounting |

### Step 6: Scale to Affiliates (If Ready)

If the business has 50+ customers and a proven referral system, consider formal affiliate partnerships:

1. **Identify complementary businesses** (same customer, different product)
2. **Propose value exchange** (reciprocal referrals or commission)
3. **Provide affiliate assets** (landing pages, email swipe, social copy)
4. **Set commission structure** (20-50% of first sale, or flat fee per lead)
5. **Track and pay promptly** (nothing kills affiliate relationships faster than late payments)
