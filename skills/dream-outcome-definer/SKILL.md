# Dream Outcome Definer

Identify the single aspirational end-state your customer is buying, pressure-test it against Hormozi's status-driver framework, and output a dream outcome statement that becomes the foundation of your entire offer.

Read `references/frameworks/value-equation-explained.md` for the full Value Equation framework. Read `references/frameworks/grand-slam-offer-anatomy.md` for how Dream Outcome fits into the complete offer structure.

## Why This Matters

The dream outcome is not what your product does. It is the end-state your customer wants to achieve. Hormozi puts it this way:

> "The dream outcome is what separates whether someone's even interested in your category of offer or not."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

Most businesses describe features or processes. The dream outcome is the emotional, status-laden result. A gym doesn't sell workouts; it sells the moment someone takes off their shirt at the pool without shame. A B2B agency doesn't sell marketing services; it sells the founder's ability to say their revenue number at a dinner party.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Describe What You Sell

Ask the user:

1. **What is your product or service?** (Be specific: coaching program, SaaS tool, physical product, done-for-you service, etc.)
2. **Who is your ideal customer?** (Demographics, psychographics, current pain)
3. **What result do they get from working with you?** (The literal, functional outcome)

### Step 2: Identify the Deep Driver

Hormozi identifies that all purchasing decisions ultimately tie to status:

> "Men in general probably want to make more money, women in general usually want to look better, because both of those are more associated with status."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

Map the user's functional outcome to one of the four deep drivers:

| Deep Driver | Status Expression | Common Categories |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Money** | "I earn more than my peers" | B2B services, investing, career coaching, business education |
| **Appearance** | "I look better than my peers" | Fitness, beauty, fashion, cosmetic procedures |
| **Relationships** | "I am desired/respected by others" | Dating, social skills, leadership, parenting |
| **Health** | "I will live longer/better than my peers" | Medical, supplements, wellness, therapy |

Ask: "When your customer gets this result, what do they really get to say about themselves? What status shift occurs?"

### Step 3: B2B vs. B2C Category Check

Apply Hormozi's pricing logic:

> "Why is it that B2B offers tend to be more expensive than B2C offers? Because it's more closely tied to ROI."
> — Alex Hormozi, "The $100M Offer Formula"

| Category | Dream Outcome Must Be Tied To | Price Ceiling Determined By |
|----------|-------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **B2B** | Measurable ROI (revenue, savings, time) | Multiple of the ROI delivered |
| **B2C** | Status proxy (how others perceive them) | Emotional urgency of the transformation |

If B2B: the dream outcome must be expressible as a number. "We'll grow your business" fails. "Add $240,000 in annual revenue" works. Hormozi's gym launch averaged exactly this:

> "The average gym that worked with us added $240,000 a year in top line... 3.1x their profit, from $2,950/month take-home to $8,900/month."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Create A GRAND SLAM Offer"

If B2C: the dream outcome must be expressible as a before/after. "You'll feel better" fails. "Wake up after liposuction and be thinner" works.

### Step 4: The $5 vs. $50,000 Test

Hormozi uses this test to validate whether the dream outcome is specific enough:

> "How is it that you can have a $5 PDF and a $50,000 liposuction thing, but they both fundamentally solve the same problem — they don't like the way their stomach looks? The difference is the other three variables."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

Ask: "Within your category, what's the cheapest version of this solution and the most expensive? Where does your offer sit, and why?"

If the user can't articulate why their offer is different from the $5 version, the dream outcome isn't specific enough. Push them to get more concrete about the transformation.

## Output

Generate a **Dream Outcome Brief** with this structure:

```markdown
## Dream Outcome Brief

**Business:** [their business]
**Customer:** [their ideal customer]

### Dream Outcome Statement
[One sentence: "My customer's dream outcome is to [specific end-state] so that [status driver]"]

### Category Check
- **B2B / B2C:** [which]
- **Deep Driver:** [money / appearance / relationships / health]
- **Status Expression:** [what they get to say/feel about themselves]

### Specificity Score
- **Functional clarity:** [1-10] Can you measure when someone achieves this?
- **Emotional resonance:** [1-10] Does this make the prospect's heart rate increase?
- **Differentiation:** [1-10] Does this sound different from every other offer in your space?

### $5 vs. $50,000 Positioning
- **$5 version:** [cheapest way to get this result]
- **$50,000 version:** [most premium way to get this result]
- **Your position:** [where you sit and why]

### Next Step
→ Feed this dream outcome into `/likelihood-booster` to audit your proof stack
→ Or run the full Value Equation with `/value-equation-scorer`
```
