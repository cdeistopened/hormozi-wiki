# Price Increase Calculator

Design a price increase rollout plan: announce, clear the pipeline, introduce the new price with anchor-high positioning, manage sales team conviction, and weather the adjustment period.

Read `references/frameworks/pricing-psychology.md` for the anchor-high methodology. Read `references/frameworks/four-core-objections.md` for handling price objections that increase during transitions.

## Why This Matters

Raising prices is one of the highest-leverage moves a business can make, and also one of the most feared. Hormozi's experience across multiple businesses and industries shows the fear is almost always unfounded:

> "99 times out of 100 it's purely in your head and in your sales team's head."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How I RAISE PRICES without losing sales"

The process has a specific order. Skip steps and you get resistance. Follow the process and you get a cash flow bump, followed by a sustainable price increase.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Assess the Situation

Ask the user:

1. **What is your current price?**
2. **What is your target price?**
3. **How long have you been at the current price?**
4. **What has changed since you last set the price?** (More proof, better product, more demand)
5. **Do you have a sales team, or do you sell yourself?**
6. **What is your current close rate?**
7. **How big is your current pipeline?** (Leads in progress, not yet closed)

### Step 2: Calculate the Price Architecture

Apply the Hormozi formula from `/price-presentation-method`:

| From → To | Price Anchor | Payment Plan | Prepay Discount |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| $1K → $2K | $3K | $1K + $2K | $2K ("save $1K") |
| $2K → $3K | $4K | $2K + $2K | $3K ("save $1K") |
| $3K → $4K | $5K | $3K + $2K | $4K ("save $1K") |
| $4K → $5K | $6K | $4K + $2K | $5K ("save $1K") |
| $6K → $8K | $10K | $6K + $4K | $8K ("save $2K") |
| $10K → $12K | $15K | $6K + $3K + $3K (or 10K + 5K) | $12K ("save $3K") |

### Step 3: Design the Rollout Calendar

Hormozi's 4-step price increase process:

**Week 1-2: Announce the Increase**

> "Always own your price increases. It's one of the easiest ways to create true scarcity and urgency."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How I RAISE PRICES without losing sales"

Tell your pipeline, your email list, and your audience:
- "We're raising our price to [$ANCHOR] on [DATE]"
- "If you've been on the fence, now is the time"
- Justify with improvements: "We have more successes, we're making it easier and faster"

**Week 2-3: Clear the Pipeline**

> "Always clear the pipe. Announce it. Give them urgency to close more deals faster and clear out the pipeline."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How I RAISE PRICES without losing sales"

This step has three benefits:
1. Creates real urgency for fence-sitters (not fake countdown timers)
2. Generates a cash flow surge before the transition
3. Eliminates future "but I heard a different price" conversations

**Week 4: Introduce the New Price**

Switch to the new price architecture. Sales team uses the anchor-high script from `/price-presentation-method`.

The first 2-4 weeks at the new price may see a dip in close rate. This is normal.

**Week 5-8: Weather the Adjustment**

> "You as the business owner get a nice boost of cash flow before you make the change so that you can weather maybe a dip in cash flow during the adjustment period, which sometimes happens."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How I RAISE PRICES without losing sales"

The cash surplus from clearing the pipeline funds the adjustment period. Close rate typically recovers within 4-6 weeks as the team builds conviction at the new price.

### Step 4: Manage Sales Team Conviction

If you have a sales team, conviction is the make-or-break factor.

> "They're going to have an emotional comfort selling around a certain price. You have to accept that."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How I RAISE PRICES without losing sales"

**Conviction-building sequence:**
1. **Announce internally first** - give the team 1-2 weeks to adjust mentally before the price goes live
2. **Provide the home base safety net** - they can still say the old number as the first payment
3. **Reframe as a discount** - "You're not asking for more. You're GIVING a discount from the anchor"
4. **Celebrate early wins** - first closes at the new price get team-wide recognition
5. **Track and share data** - show that the close rate is recovering

### Step 5: Supply-Side Price Leverage

For businesses with capacity constraints, Hormozi adds the supply-demand lever:

> "When demand increases, cut supply."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Charge Exorbitant Prices"

If you're at or near capacity:
- Raise the price AND reduce availability
- Introduce a waitlist
- Create a premium tier with limited spots
- Let the scarcity be real, not manufactured
