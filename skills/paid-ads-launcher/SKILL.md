# Paid Ads Launcher

Determine if you're ready for paid ads, then design your first campaign using Hormozi's data-driven creative methodology and his paid advertising principles.

Read `references/frameworks/100m-leads-core-four.md` for how paid ads fit into the Core Four. Read `references/frameworks/content-as-lead-gen.md` for how content and ads work together.

## Why This Matters

Paid ads are the fourth channel in Hormozi's Core Four, and he is explicit about when to use them: AFTER you have a proven offer. Running ads to an unproven offer is burning money.

> "If a business cannot pay $100-200 to get someone in the door, they don't have a marketing problem, they have a business problem."
> — Alex Hormozi, "4 Steps to Maximizing Revenue Generated Per Customer"

Hormozi ran ads that generated over 106 million impressions. His secret was not the ad platform. It was the creative - specifically, using data to generate hooks that stop the scroll.

> "One in 5.5 gyms is a million dollar gym. Every 7.7 days we create another seven-figure gym. 70% of clients hit over 16 pounds lost."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How I Got 106M Impressions With Ads"

Each of those hooks comes from the same data set, sliced differently. The Data Hook Generator methodology (three wheels: percentage/fraction, outcome measured, time duration) lets you create 15-20 hooks from a single proof point.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Readiness Check

Ask the user:

1. **Have you sold your offer to at least 10 people via organic/outreach?** (If no: stop. Go to `/warm-outreach-system` first.)
2. **What is your average customer value?** (Lifetime, not just first purchase)
3. **What can you afford to spend to acquire one customer?** (Should be <1/3 of customer value)
4. **What is your monthly ad budget?** (Minimum $1,000/mo to test meaningfully)
5. **Do you have proof/results you can reference?** (Testimonials, data, case studies)

**Kill switch:** If they haven't sold to 10 people organically, DO NOT help them run ads. Send them to outreach first. Hormozi is explicit:

> "Given your financial situation, it's going to be making content and reaching out to people - that's it."
> — Alex Hormozi, "I Helped Business Owners Overcome Their Fear of Failure"

### Step 2: Choose the Platform

| Platform | Best For | Minimum Budget | Key Metric |
|----------|---------|----------------|------------|
| **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)** | B2C, local, e-commerce, coaches | $1,000/mo | Cost per lead |
| **Google (Search)** | High-intent buyers, services, SaaS | $1,500/mo | Cost per click → conversion |
| **YouTube** | Education, authority, longer sales cycle | $2,000/mo | Cost per view → lead |
| **LinkedIn** | B2B, enterprise, recruiting | $3,000/mo | Cost per lead (higher CPM) |
| **TikTok** | Younger demo, attention, brand awareness | $1,000/mo | Cost per click |

Match platform to where their ideal customer spends time AND has buying intent.

### Step 3: Design the Creative

Apply the Data Hook methodology:

**Wheel 1 - The Fraction:** How to express your proof
- Top X% of clients...
- 1 in Y customers...
- The average client...
- X out of Y people who...

**Wheel 2 - The Outcome:** What you measure
- Revenue generated
- Weight lost
- Time saved
- Conversion rate
- Customer result metric

**Wheel 3 - The Timeframe:** When it happened
- In their first 30 days
- Within 6 months
- Every single week
- Since we started tracking

**Generate 10+ hooks** by spinning these three wheels against the user's actual data. Each hook becomes the opening line (or headline) of an ad.

**Ad structure:**
1. **Hook** (data-driven, from the wheels above)
2. **Problem** (1-2 sentences: what the prospect is struggling with)
3. **Solution** (what you do, briefly)
4. **Proof** (specific result, testimonial snippet, or data point)
5. **CTA** (clear, low-friction: "Comment X below" or "Click to get [lead magnet]")

### Step 4: Set the Testing Framework

| Element | Test Method | Sample Size Needed |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Hook/headline** | 3-5 variations | 1,000 impressions each |
| **Creative (image/video)** | 2-3 variations | 1,000 impressions each |
| **Audience** | 2-3 targeting sets | $200-500 per audience |
| **Offer/CTA** | A/B test after above | 100+ clicks per variant |

Test in order: hook first (biggest impact), then creative, then audience, then offer. Hormozi's approach is to test creative aggressively and let the platform optimize targeting.

### Step 5: Set Budget and Timeline

**Week 1-2:** Testing phase
- Budget: 40% of monthly budget
- Run 3-5 hook variants
- Kill losers after 1,000 impressions, scale winners

**Week 3-4:** Optimization phase
- Budget: 60% of monthly budget
- Double down on winning hooks
- Test creative variants of top hooks
- Measure cost per lead and cost per acquisition

**Month 2+:** Scaling phase
- If CPA is profitable: increase budget 20% per week
- If CPA is too high: return to creative testing
- Add retargeting campaigns for website visitors and lead magnet consumers
