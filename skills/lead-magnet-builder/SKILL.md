# Lead Magnet Builder

Walk the user through Hormozi's 9-step lead magnet creation process and output a complete lead magnet brief ready for production.

Read `references/frameworks/lead-magnet-design-principles.md` for the full framework. Read `references/frameworks/100m-leads-core-four.md` for how lead magnets fit into the broader lead generation system.

## Why This Matters

Most lead magnets fail because they solve a vague problem for a vague audience. Hormozi's method forces specificity at every step:

> "There's nine steps... what and who, how to deliver, how to solve it, name it, make it easy, make it good, create the next steps."
> — Alex Hormozi, "$100M Leads - Lead Magnet Mastery"

The lead magnet is not the product. It is the proof that your product works. Hormozi frames it as a prediction problem:

> "The reason people buy is based on a prediction of future value. So they buy because they predict that they will get value from you later. What is the best way to predict what someone will do for you in the future? What they've done for you in the past."
> — Alex Hormozi, "Giving Away Free Stuff Will Make You Rich"

A lead magnet that delivers real value creates a data point: this person can help me. That data point makes the paid offer easier to sell.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Identify the Engaged Lead

Ask the user:

1. **What is your core paid offer?** (The thing you ultimately want to sell)
2. **Who is your ideal customer?** (Be specific: demographics, psychographics, current pain)
3. **What would make someone a "warm" lead for your offer?** (What action or interest signals they're a buyer?)

The lead magnet must attract people who are likely to buy the core offer. A gym doesn't want anyone who wants to be healthier. It wants people who want to lose weight in the next 90 days and are willing to pay for help.

### Step 2: Determine What Problem to Solve

Hormozi's key insight: the lead magnet should solve a narrow, complete problem - not give a taste of the full solution.

> "A lead magnet should solve a narrow problem completely rather than a broad problem incompletely."
> — Alex Hormozi, "$100M Leads - Lead Magnet Mastery"

Map the user's core offer to a "one step" problem:
- What is ONE specific problem their ideal customer has right now?
- Can it be solved completely with a free resource?
- Does solving this problem naturally create awareness of the bigger problem (the one the paid offer solves)?

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Solve step 1 of a multi-step process | Weight loss: "7-Day Meal Plan" (step 1 of losing weight) |
| Solve one painful symptom | B2B: "Email Template Pack" (solves cold outreach pain, reveals they need a full system) |
| Diagnose the problem | Consulting: "Business Audit Scorecard" (reveals gaps the service fills) |
| Deliver a quick win | SaaS: "5-Minute Setup Guide" (gets them using the tool, want more) |

### Step 3: Match Magnet to Audience Sophistication

Ask: "How aware is your audience of their problem and the solutions available?"

| Awareness Level | Lead Magnet Type | Why |
|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| Unaware | Educational content (video, blog, quiz) | They need to understand the problem first |
| Problem-aware | Diagnostic tool (scorecard, audit, quiz) | They know the pain, need clarity on the cause |
| Solution-aware | Template/toolkit/checklist | They know what to do, need help doing it |
| Product-aware | Free trial, sample, case study | They're comparing options, need proof |

### Step 4: Choose the Delivery Vehicle

Hormozi identifies seven delivery vehicles:

| Vehicle | Effort to Create | Consumption Speed | Best For |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Software/tool** | High | Instant | SaaS, tech audiences |
| **Information (PDF/guide)** | Low | 5-30 min | Most businesses |
| **Services (audit/call)** | Medium (per lead) | 15-60 min | High-ticket B2B |
| **Physical product (sample)** | High (COGS) | Varies | E-commerce, physical |
| **Access (community/group)** | Medium | Ongoing | Community-based businesses |
| **Video/course** | Medium | 15-60 min | Education, coaching |
| **Quiz/assessment** | Medium | 2-5 min | Any (highest opt-in rates) |

### Step 5: Create the Solution

The solution must be genuinely valuable. Hormozi's test:

> "Would someone pay for this? If the answer is yes, it's a good lead magnet. If the answer is no, it's not good enough."
> — Alex Hormozi, "$100M Leads - Lead Magnet Mastery"

Build the content that solves the narrow problem completely. Every claim should be specific. Every instruction should be actionable. No filler.

### Step 6: Name It

Apply Hormozi's naming formula:

**[Result] + [Timeframe] + [Container]**

Examples:
- "The 21-Day Meal Plan" (result: meals handled, timeframe: 21 days, container: plan)
- "5 Email Templates That Book Calls" (result: booked calls, timeframe: implied fast, container: templates)
- "The 10-Minute Business Audit" (result: business diagnosis, timeframe: 10 minutes, container: audit)

Bad names describe the format. Good names describe the outcome.

### Step 7: Make It Easy to Consume

The lead magnet should take less time to consume than the prospect expects. If they expect 30 minutes, it should take 10. Hormozi's principle:

> "Make it easy to consume. If it takes too long, they won't finish it, and if they don't finish it, they don't get the result, and if they don't get the result, they don't buy."
> — Alex Hormozi, "$100M Leads - Lead Magnet Mastery"

Apply the consumption test:
- Can someone consume this in one sitting?
- Does every section advance them toward the result?
- Can you cut 30% of the content without losing the outcome?

### Step 8: Make It Genuinely Valuable

This is where most lead magnets fail. They hold back value to "save it for the paid offer." Hormozi's counter-intuitive principle:

> "Give away the secrets, sell the implementation."
> — Alex Hormozi, "Giving Away Free Stuff Will Make You Rich"

The free thing should work. If someone takes your lead magnet and gets the result without buying, that is a success - because they will tell others, and the people they tell will buy.

### Step 9: Design the Next Step

The lead magnet must create a natural bridge to the paid offer. The bridge is not a hard sell. It is the logical next step.

| Lead Magnet Outcome | Natural Bridge | Paid Offer |
|---------------------|---------------|------------|
| "Now I know my problem" | "Here's how to fix it completely" | Done-for-you service |
| "I got a quick win" | "Here's how to get the full result" | Course or coaching |
| "I see what's possible" | "Here's how to do it faster" | Premium program |

The bridge should feel inevitable, not promotional.

## Output

Generate a **Lead Magnet Brief** with this structure:

```markdown
## Lead Magnet Brief

**Business:** [their business]
**Core Paid Offer:** [what they sell]
**Ideal Customer:** [who they're targeting]

### The Lead Magnet
- **Problem Solved:** [specific narrow problem]
- **Name:** [Result + Timeframe + Container]
- **Delivery Vehicle:** [which of the 7]
- **Consumption Time:** [how long to complete]

### Content Outline
1. [Section 1 - what it covers]
2. [Section 2 - what it covers]
3. [Section 3 - what it covers]
[...]

### Value Test
- **Would someone pay for this?** [yes/no + why]
- **Does it solve the narrow problem completely?** [yes/no]
- **Does it naturally lead to the paid offer?** [yes/no + how]

### Bridge to Core Offer
- **What the prospect realizes after consuming:** [insight that creates demand]
- **Natural next step language:** [the CTA that feels inevitable]

### Naming Alternatives
1. [Option A]
2. [Option B]
3. [Option C]

### Next Step
→ Use `/core-four-channel-selector` to decide how to distribute this lead magnet
→ Use `/content-engine-blueprint` to build content around the lead magnet
```
