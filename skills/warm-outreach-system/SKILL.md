# Warm Outreach System

Design a warm outreach campaign targeting people who already know you - personal contacts, past customers, professional network - with exact scripts, volume targets, and follow-up sequences.

Read `references/frameworks/warm-vs-cold-outreach.md` for when to use warm vs. cold outreach. Read `references/frameworks/100m-leads-core-four.md` for how warm outreach fits into the full lead generation system.

## Why This Matters

Warm outreach is Hormozi's #1 recommended starting point for any business. The conversion rate is dramatically higher because the trust variable is already partially solved.

> "Content, you reach out to people, and you do that for four straight hours every day. You reach out to 10,000 people, you'll have the business you want."
> — Alex Hormozi, "I Helped Business Owners Overcome Their Fear of Failure"

The math is simple: if you reach out to 100 people per day for 100 days, that is 10,000 contacts. At even a 1% conversion rate, that is 100 customers.

> "You're 6 months away, seriously. You got to reach out to, let's call it, 10,000 people. You reach out to 10,000 people, you'll have the business you want."

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Inventory the Network

Ask the user:

1. **How many people are in your phone contacts?** (Most people: 500-2,000)
2. **How many past customers or clients do you have?** (Even from past businesses)
3. **What social media accounts do you have and how many connections?** (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram)
4. **Are you in any professional groups, alumni networks, or communities?**
5. **What is your offer and who is the ideal customer?**

### Step 2: Segment the List

Not all warm contacts are equal. Segment into tiers:

| Tier | Who | Approach | Expected Response |
|------|-----|----------|-------------------|
| **Tier 1: Past buyers** | People who've already paid you | Re-engagement + new offer | 10-25% response |
| **Tier 2: Close network** | Friends, family, close colleagues | Personal ask + referral request | 30-50% response |
| **Tier 3: Professional network** | LinkedIn connections, industry contacts | Value-first + soft ask | 5-15% response |
| **Tier 4: Social connections** | Facebook friends, Instagram followers | Content + DM follow-up | 2-5% response |

### Step 3: Build the Scripts

Each tier gets a different script. The structure is the same:

**The ACA Framework (Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask):**

1. **Acknowledge** the relationship (how you know them)
2. **Compliment** something genuine (recent post, achievement, their work)
3. **Ask** without pitching (ask who they know, not if they want to buy)

**Tier 1 Script (Past Buyers):**
```
Hey [name], it's been a while! I was thinking about you because [specific reason related to their past purchase]. I've been working on something new that [brief description of new offer]. Would it be cool if I told you about it?
```

**Tier 2 Script (Close Network):**
```
Hey [name], I'm working on [description of business]. I'm looking for [specific type of person]. Do you know anyone who [describes ideal customer]? Would be a huge help.
```

**Tier 3 Script (Professional Network):**
```
Hey [name], I saw your [specific thing]. Really cool. Quick question: I help [type of person] get [specific result]. Do you know anyone who might be interested in that?
```

The key insight: you are NOT asking them to buy. You are asking if they KNOW someone. This removes the pressure and often leads them to self-identify as the customer.

### Step 4: Set Volume Targets

| Time Available | Daily Outreach | Weekly Total | 6-Month Total |
|---------------|---------------|--------------|---------------|
| 1 hour/day | 30-40 messages | 150-200 | 3,600-4,800 |
| 2 hours/day | 60-80 messages | 300-400 | 7,200-9,600 |
| 4 hours/day | 100-120 messages | 500-600 | 12,000-14,400 |

Hormozi's standard: 100/day for someone doing this full-time. Adjust proportionally.

### Step 5: Design the Follow-Up Sequence

Most people give up after one message. The money is in the follow-up:

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Initial outreach message |
| Day 3 | Follow-up if no response ("Hey, just bumping this up") |
| Day 7 | Value-add follow-up (share something useful, re-ask) |
| Day 14 | Final follow-up ("No worries if not, just wanted to circle back") |
| Day 30+ | Add to content nurture list (they see your posts) |

Track everything. A simple spreadsheet works: Name, Date Contacted, Response, Follow-up Date, Outcome.

### Step 6: Handle Responses

When someone responds positively, DO NOT pitch immediately. Transition to a conversation:

1. Thank them for responding
2. Ask 2-3 qualifying questions about their situation
3. If qualified, offer a free call or consultation
4. On the call, use the CLOSER framework (see `/closer-framework-runner`)

When someone refers you to another person, ALWAYS follow up and report back to the referrer. This creates a referral loop.
