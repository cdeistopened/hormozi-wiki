# Time Delay Compressor

Map every delay point between purchase and result, then generate solutions to compress each one. The output is a before/after timeline showing how your offer becomes dramatically more valuable by reducing the denominator of the Value Equation.

Read `references/frameworks/value-equation-explained.md` for the full framework. Time delay is a denominator — reducing it multiplies perceived value.

## Why This Matters

Time delay is one of the two variables in the denominator of Hormozi's Value Equation. It divides the value of the dream outcome. The longer it takes, the less someone will pay, the harder you have to sell.

> "Personal training — you got to arm wrestle somebody for an hour to get them to buy a $2,000 package because they might get their six-pack 12 to 18 months later. Whereas if you do liposuction, you're going to go to sleep and then you're going to wake up and you're going to be significantly thinner. The time delay is so much shorter, and because of that it increases the value overall."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

This is why liposuction costs $50,000 and a personal training package costs $2,000 — even though both solve the same problem. The difference is time delay.

> "If someone were able to click a button on a website and immediately look at their stomach and have a six-pack, that would be incredibly valuable. On the flip side, if it takes them two years, it's significantly less valuable."
> — Alex Hormozi, "The $100M Offer Formula"

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Map the Current Timeline

Ask the user to walk through every step between purchase and result:

1. **What happens immediately after purchase?** (Onboarding call? Email sequence? Login? Shipping?)
2. **What are the first 3 things the customer must do?**
3. **When does the customer experience their first tangible result?** (Not "feel good about buying" — an actual result they can measure or see.)
4. **When does the customer achieve the full dream outcome?**
5. **What are the biggest "waiting periods" where nothing visible happens?**

Build a sequential timeline:

```
Purchase → [Step 1: X days] → [Step 2: X days] → [First Result: Day X] → ... → [Dream Outcome: Day X]
```

### Step 2: Classify Each Delay

For each gap, classify the type of delay:

| Delay Type | Example | Compression Strategy |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| **Onboarding delay** | "We schedule a kickoff call within 2 weeks" | Same-day automated onboarding, pre-built templates |
| **Learning delay** | "Customer needs to learn the system first" | Done-for-you setup, guided walkthrough, skip-to-action |
| **Dependency delay** | "Waiting for customer to provide info" | Pre-purchase questionnaire, intake form as part of sales |
| **Processing delay** | "Takes 4-6 weeks to see results" | Instant-win deliverable while they wait, milestone markers |
| **Skill delay** | "Customer needs practice/reps" | Pre-built assets, templates, swipe files, done-with-you |
| **External delay** | "Waiting on a third party" | Manage the third party for them, pre-negotiate, automate |

### Step 3: Generate Compression Solutions

For each delay point, brainstorm using Hormozi's method — look at "what happens immediately before and immediately post purchase" and solve those micro-steps:

> "It's really getting granular about all the little steps that have to occur in order for someone to get a result. Looking at what happens immediately before and immediately post purchase, all the little steps, and then trying to deconstruct the offer."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

**The 6 compression levers:**

1. **Pre-built assets:** What can you create once and give to every customer? (Templates, checklists, starter kits)
2. **Done-for-you components:** What steps can you do for them instead of teaching them? (Setup, configuration, first draft)
3. **Automation:** What steps can software replace? (Onboarding sequences, data collection, scheduling)
4. **Instant-win deliverables:** What quick result can you deliver in the first 24-48 hours? (Quick audit, first iteration, diagnostic report)
5. **Parallel processing:** What steps currently happen sequentially that could happen simultaneously?
6. **Elimination:** What steps aren't actually necessary for the result? Remove them entirely.

### Step 4: Build the Compressed Timeline

Create a new timeline showing the compressed version side-by-side with the original.
