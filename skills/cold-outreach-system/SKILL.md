# Cold Outreach System Builder

Design a cold outreach system - cold email, cold DM, or cold call - with targeting criteria, list building approach, messaging templates, and a volume ramp plan.

Read `references/frameworks/warm-vs-cold-outreach.md` for when to use cold vs. warm. Read `references/frameworks/100m-leads-core-four.md` for how cold outreach fits into the full system.

## Why This Matters

Cold outreach is how you reach people who have never heard of you. It is harder than warm outreach (lower conversion rates) but infinitely more scalable. Hormozi's approach is simple: volume solves most problems.

> "Do twice the activity... the biggest thing that I've seen work repeatedly is just doing twice the amount of the activity."
> — Alex Hormozi, "Sales Was Hard Until I Understood These 9 Concepts"

Cold outreach fails when people treat it like warm outreach. The prospect does not know you, does not trust you, and did not ask to hear from you. Every word must earn the next second of their attention.

## The Consultation

### Step 1: Define the Target

Ask the user:

1. **Who is your ideal customer?** (Industry, company size, role/title, geography)
2. **What specific problem do you solve for them?** (Must be concrete and measurable)
3. **What is your offer and price point?**
4. **What result can you guarantee or demonstrate?** (Case studies, data, proof)
5. **Which channel do you prefer?** (Cold email, cold DM, cold call, or all three)

### Step 2: Build the List

The list is everything. A great message to the wrong person gets zero response. A mediocre message to the right person still works.

| List Source | Best For | Cost |
|------------|---------|------|
| LinkedIn Sales Navigator | B2B by title/company/industry | $80-100/mo |
| Apollo.io / ZoomInfo | B2B email + phone data | $50-500/mo |
| Instagram/Twitter search | B2C or creator/personal brands | Free |
| Industry directories | Niche B2B (dentists, gyms, agencies) | Free-$200 |
| Facebook groups | Community-based targeting | Free |
| Google Maps / Yelp | Local businesses | Free |

**Targeting filters to apply:**
- Company size (revenue or employees)
- Geographic region (if relevant)
- Tech stack or tools used (if relevant)
- Recent triggers (hiring, funding, growth signals)

### Step 3: Craft the Message

Cold outreach messages follow a strict structure. Every unnecessary word reduces response rate.

**The Cold Email Framework:**

```
Subject: [Result] for [their company/role]

Hey [name],

[1 sentence: observation about their business that proves you did research]

[1-2 sentences: the result you deliver, with a specific number or proof point]

[1 sentence: low-friction CTA - question, not a meeting request]

[your name]
```

**Key principles:**
- Subject line: 3-5 words, implies relevance
- Opening: prove you know them (not "I hope this email finds you well")
- Body: lead with the result, not your process
- CTA: ask a question, not for a meeting. "Would this be relevant?" beats "Can we schedule 15 minutes?"

**Cold DM Framework:**
```
[Compliment/observation about their recent post or content]
[Bridge to the problem you solve]
[Question that opens a conversation]
```

DMs must be shorter than email. 2-3 sentences maximum. No links in the first message.

**Cold Call Framework:**
```
"Hey [name], this is [your name] with [company]. I know I'm calling out of the blue - do you have 30 seconds?"
[If yes]: "I help [type of business] [specific result]. I'm reaching out because [reason they specifically]. Is that something you'd want to hear about?"
[If interested]: "Great - what does your [relevant process] look like right now?"
```

### Step 4: Set Volume and Cadence

| Channel | Daily Volume (Solo) | Daily Volume (With Tools) | Response Rate Target |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Cold email | 50-100/day | 200-500/day (with warmup) | 2-5% reply rate |
| Cold DM | 30-50/day | 50-100/day | 3-8% reply rate |
| Cold call | 50-80 dials/day | N/A | 2-5% conversation rate |

**Ramp plan:**
- Week 1: 25% of target volume (test messaging)
- Week 2: 50% of target volume (refine based on responses)
- Week 3: 75% of target volume
- Week 4+: Full volume, optimize from data

### Step 5: Build the Follow-Up Sequence

A single touchpoint almost never converts cold. Build a multi-touch sequence:

| Touch | Timing | Channel | Content |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Primary (email/DM) | Initial outreach |
| 2 | Day 3 | Same channel | Different angle, same offer |
| 3 | Day 7 | Same channel | Case study or proof point |
| 4 | Day 10 | Different channel (call/DM) | Brief, direct check-in |
| 5 | Day 14 | Primary | "Break-up" email (last touch) |

The break-up email often gets the highest response rate because it creates scarcity.

### Step 6: Track and Optimize

| Metric | Target | Action if Below |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Open rate (email) | 40%+ | Fix subject line |
| Reply rate | 3%+ | Fix message body |
| Positive reply rate | 30%+ of replies | Fix targeting |
| Booking rate | 50%+ of positives | Fix CTA / booking process |
| Close rate | 20%+ of meetings | Fix offer (use `/closer-framework-runner`) |
