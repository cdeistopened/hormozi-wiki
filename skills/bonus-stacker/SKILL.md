# Bonus Stack Builder

Map each customer objection to a bonus that resolves it, then sequence the bonuses using Hormozi's "sell, then stack" choreography — offer the core first, reveal bonuses as objection-handlers during the sale, and deliver remaining bonuses as surprise-and-delight post-purchase.

Read `references/frameworks/bonus-stacking-psychology.md` for the full psychology behind why this works and case studies from Hormozi's businesses.

## Why This Matters

Most businesses handle objections by lowering the price. Hormozi's method is the opposite: add value instead of subtracting price.

> "This also allows the sales team to stop doing discounts in order to close people. We just add value rather than taking away price."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

The choreography matters as much as the bonuses themselves. You don't dump all 7 bonuses on the prospect at once. You sell the core offer first. If they object, you deploy a bonus that directly addresses that objection. Remaining bonuses become post-purchase surprises.

> "The idea is that you make the ask on the initial offer. If they say no, you figure out the constraint, and then you plug that bonus in. And then maybe you need to put three bonuses in in order to get them over the edge."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

For fast buyers who don't need the bonuses to close:

> "If you get the fast buyer that doesn't need the bonuses, you just give them the bonuses and they love you. And if you got somebody who needs all seven, then you give them the seven bonuses on the sales calls."
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

## The Consultation

### Step 1: List the Core Offer

Ask the user:
1. **What is your core offer?** (The main thing they're buying)
2. **What is the price?**
3. **What result does it deliver?**

### Step 2: Surface All Objections

Ask the user to list every objection, concern, fear, or hesitation they hear (or anticipate) from prospects:

Common objection categories:
- **"I don't have time"** — Time/effort concern
- **"I've tried this before and it didn't work"** — Likelihood/trust concern
- **"What if it doesn't work for MY situation?"** — Specificity concern
- **"I can't afford it"** — Price-to-value concern (NOT actually about money)
- **"I need to think about it"** — Risk/commitment concern
- **"My spouse/partner/boss won't approve"** — Authority concern
- **"I don't know enough about X to make it work"** — Skill/knowledge gap
- **"What about [specific technical obstacle]?"** — Implementation concern

### Step 3: Design a Bonus for Each Objection

For each objection, create a bonus that directly resolves it:

| Objection | Bonus | Format | Creation Effort |
|-----------|-------|--------|----------------|
| "I don't have time" | Done-for-you templates / shortcuts / checklists | PDF/digital | Low |
| "I've tried and failed" | Case study library showing people like them succeeding | Video/PDF | Medium |
| "What about my specific situation?" | 1-on-1 audit / custom plan session | Live call | High |
| "I don't know enough about X" | Training module on X / quick-start guide | Video course | Medium |
| "What if it doesn't work?" | Extended guarantee / extra support window | Service commitment | Low |

**Bonus design principles:**
- Each bonus should be nameworthy (give it a compelling name — see `/offer-namer`)
- Each bonus should have a perceived value higher than its creation cost
- Each bonus should be genuinely useful, not filler
- The best bonuses are things you've already created (recordings, templates, tools)

### Step 4: Assign Deployment Choreography

Split bonuses into three categories:

**Category A: Pre-Close Reserves (2-3 bonuses)**
These are your objection-handlers. The salesperson holds these in reserve and deploys them when a specific objection arises.

**Category B: Close Sweeteners (1-2 bonuses)**
These are mentioned as part of the close to tip undecided prospects. "And on top of all that, you also get..."

**Category C: Post-Purchase Surprises (remaining bonuses)**
These are given AFTER the sale as surprise-and-delight. They increase satisfaction, reduce buyer's remorse, and generate referrals.

> "Post-purchase, in order to make sure that ops is all the same, you then give them a surprise and delay with the remaining four. You say, 'By the way, since you did buy, I want to give you these other things.'"
> — Alex Hormozi, "How To Craft A $100M Offer In 6 Minutes"

### Step 5: Value-Stack the Total

Calculate the perceived value of the entire stack. The total perceived value should be 3-10x the asking price.

## Output

Generate a **Bonus Stack Design**:

```markdown
## Bonus Stack Design

**Core Offer:** [their offer]
**Core Price:** $[X]

### Objection-to-Bonus Map

| # | Objection | Bonus Name | Format | Perceived Value | Deployment |
|---|-----------|-----------|--------|----------------|------------|
| 1 | [objection] | [bonus name] | [format] | $[value] | A/B/C |
| 2 | [objection] | [bonus name] | [format] | $[value] | A/B/C |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

### Sales Call Choreography

**Opening pitch:** Present core offer only at $[price]

**If objection → deploy reserve:**
- Objection: "[objection 1]" → Deploy: [Bonus Name 1]
- Objection: "[objection 2]" → Deploy: [Bonus Name 2]
- Objection: "[objection 3]" → Deploy: [Bonus Name 3]

**Close sweetener:** "And on top of everything, you also get [Bonus Name 4] and [Bonus Name 5]"

**Post-purchase surprise:** Within 24 hours, deliver [Bonus Name 6] and [Bonus Name 7] with message: "Since you joined, I wanted to give you these as well..."

### Total Value Stack
- Core offer: $[X]
- Bonus 1: $[X]
- Bonus 2: $[X]
- ...
- **Total perceived value: $[sum]**
- **Your price: $[price]**
- **Value-to-price ratio: [X]x**

### Sales Script Language
> "So here's what you get: [core offer description]. Now, I know you mentioned [objection]. That's exactly why we also include [bonus name], which [what it does]. And because you're [getting started today / joining this cohort / one of the first X], you also get [sweetener bonus]. The total value of everything is $[total], but your investment today is just $[price]."

### Next Step
→ Add scarcity and urgency: `/scarcity-urgency-builder`
→ Name and package the full offer: `/offer-namer`
```
