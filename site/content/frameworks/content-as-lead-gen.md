## When to Use

Use this when you're:
- Wondering whether content marketing is worth the time for your business
- Posting content but not getting customers from it
- Trying to decide which platform to focus on
- Confused about whether to do brand content or direct response content
- In the early stages of a business and being told to "build an audience"

## The Framework

### The Two Objectives

Hormozi identifies exactly two objectives for business content. Every piece of content serves one or the other:

1. **Lead Nurture** — Warming up people who already know you
2. **Lead Generation** — Attracting new people who discover you

> "The purpose of having the organic content is lead nurture, not lead generation."
> — Alex Hormozi, "1.2M Followers in 6 Months"

Most early-stage businesses should use content for nurture, not generation. Content supports outreach in the early phases. It does not replace it until you invest seriously in production.

### The 5 Phases

> "And in this video, I want to break down the shift that I've had in terms of content marketing and its role within a business, the five phases in building a content marketing machine, and how to focus on one of two primary objectives with content marketing."
> — Alex Hormozi, "1.2M Followers in 6 Months"

**Phase 1: Post Something**

> "Phase one is you make something and you post it. And believe it or not, a lot of you guys haven't even done phase one."
> — Alex Hormozi, "1.2M Followers in 6 Months"

Just start. Quality doesn't matter yet. Consistency doesn't matter yet. The only goal is to overcome the inertia of never having posted. Post once. Then post again.

**Phase 2: Post Consistently**

Pick one platform. Post on a regular schedule (daily or 3-5x/week). The goal is building the habit and developing your content muscle. Do not invest in production value. Do not try to go viral.

At this phase, content is exclusively lead nurture: your outreach targets see your posts and develop familiarity and trust before you reach out (or after).

> "Phase one and phase two, if you're in one of those camps, you don't need to do anything else."
> — Alex Hormozi, "1.2M Followers in 6 Months"

**Phase 3: Post on All Platforms**

Cross-post or repurpose to 2-3 platforms. Post on a regular cadence. Start tracking what resonates (saves, shares, comments, DMs).

> "Phase three, which is your posting once a week or on some cadence on all platforms."
> — Alex Hormozi, "1.2M Followers in 6 Months"

At this phase, content starts functioning as lead generation (new people discover you) in addition to nurture.

**Phase 4: Invest in Production**

Hire help. Build a repurposing system (one long-form piece becomes 5-10 short-form pieces). Invest in quality: better thumbnails, better hooks, better editing.

This is where content starts becoming a real marketing channel, not just a support tool.

**Phase 5: Full Content Machine**

Full team. Daily content across all platforms. Data-driven optimization. A/B testing hooks and formats. Content is your primary growth engine.

> "If you want to make content marketing your actual lead generation machine, then you need to invest in it the same way you invest in it for paid marketing."
> — Alex Hormozi, "1.2M Followers in 6 Months"

### "Give in Public, Sell in Private"

This is Hormozi's content philosophy distilled to one line:

> "Give in public, sell in private."
> — Alex Hormozi, "I Helped Business Owners Overcome Their Fear of Failure"

All public content is value: education, entertainment, inspiration, documentation. The selling happens in DMs, on calls, in private conversations. When you mix selling into public content, you lose the trust that makes the private selling work.

### Content as Lead Nurture vs. Lead Generation

| Aspect | Lead Nurture (Phases 1-2) | Lead Generation (Phases 3-5) |
|--------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Audience** | People who already know you | New people discovering you |
| **Goal** | Build familiarity and trust | Attract and convert strangers |
| **Effort** | 20% of time | 50%+ of time (requires investment) |
| **Metric** | DM conversations, outreach response rate | New followers, leads, conversions |
| **Content type** | Share wins, insights, behind-the-scenes | Hook-driven, problem-solving, platform-optimized |

## Example: Phase-Appropriate Strategy

**Phase 1-2 (New coaching business, 0-500 followers):**
- Post 1x/day on LinkedIn
- Share what you're learning, client wins (with permission), honest reflections
- Spend 80% of time on outreach, 20% on content
- Content makes outreach easier: prospects see your posts before you message them

**Phase 3-4 ($50K+/mo, 5K+ followers):**
- Post daily on LinkedIn + repurpose to Twitter and Instagram
- Hire a VA to help with scheduling and repurposing
- Track which posts generate DMs and leads
- Content becomes 50% of lead generation

**Phase 5 ($200K+/mo, 50K+ followers):**
- Full content team (writer, editor, designer)
- Daily long-form + 5-10 short-form pieces
- Data-driven: test hooks, formats, CTAs
- Content is the primary growth engine

## Output

After reading this framework, you should be able to:
1. Identify which of the 5 content phases you're in
2. Set the correct objective for your phase (nurture vs. generation)
3. Avoid doing phase-4 tactics in phase-1
4. Understand when content replaces outreach and when it supports it
